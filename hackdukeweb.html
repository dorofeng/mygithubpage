The goal of HackDuke Education is to foster the spread of knowledge of anything tech and CS-related by bringing in speakers from across the country and hosting workshops. As the Director of Design of HackDuke, I was in charge of designing and helping develop a website to promote our events.



The Challenge:


We are currently in the stage of rebranding the overarching HackDuke organization for the fall, and a challenge I faced in the design of the education website was the lack of a solid branding guide. We did not want to use previous branding, and we have not finalized the rebranding so we could design the website to reflect the new brand. We also fought with time constraints to push out the website within a week. As a result, we decided to create a single page with only the most relevant information. I chose a visually pleasing (but generally neutral) color scheme and included large abstract shapes that hint at our plans for the overall rebrand. I focused primarily on maintaining style consistency within the page and displaying content in the most easy-to-read manner. The end result is a responsive and simple but effective that showcases our speakers.


The Tools:


Web and mobile wireframes were built using Sketch. The website was built with Bootstrap. I used HTML and CSS to finetune the website and to create interaction effects on the page.
