As the sole Digital Artist in Residence for Duke's Multimedia Project Studio, I am collaborating with Duke OIT to pilot a new program to connect designers with student organizations and units working through the Innovation Colab through grant. The Innovation Co-Lab is a subset of Duke OIT that provides resources for student entrepreneurs. The grant program provides funding for students who want to create a project-- most of the recipients are students interested in creating their own startup. This program has existed for several years-- however, one consistently occuring problem was that the startups were formed by students without any design experience, and so although they had incredible ideas, they had a difficult time visualizing their product and implementing it. My role is to meet up with these students and work as their temporary head UI/UX designer. Echo was my first client.
One challenge about this program is that the student startups are still work-in-progresses. The students have a fairly detailed idea about the goals and the basic functionalities of their project, and some have attempted to build wireframes, but for the most part there are still many undecided features. This means that my designer-client relationship is not simply delegation of work, but I am also advising students and making decisions about the flows, functionalities, and features of their project.



The Challenge:


Echo is a social media app whose goal is to connect people using the power of the human voice. Users anonymously upload short voice recordings of their thoughts, opinions, or stories that the Echo community can then interact with through comments or direct instant messaging conversations. My job is to design a logo and the layout for the rest of the app. The client wanted the app to be illustration-heavy to help convey a fun, warm, and inviting atmosphere.


The Tools:


Web and mobile wireframes were drawn out and then built using Sketch. Illustrations are created using Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop.
